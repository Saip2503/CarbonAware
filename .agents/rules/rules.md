# Challenge Rules & Evaluation

## 1. Critical Rules
* A maximum of **3 attempts** are allowed.
* The repository size must be strictly less than **10 MB**.
* The GitHub repository must be **public**.
* The repository must contain **only one branch**.
* **Penalty:** Failure to follow these rules may result in your submission not being evaluated.

## 2. Challenge Expectations
* Build a smart, dynamic assistant.
* Demonstrate logical decision-making based on user context.
* Ensure practical and real-world usability.
* Write clean and maintainable code.

## 3. Evaluation Parameters
Our evaluation looks at different parts of your submission. Use these tags to guide your focus:

### High Impact
*These are the most important parts of your project. Doing great here heavily drives a high overall score, while a miss will drastically lower your standing.*
* **Problem Statement Alignment:** Measures how accurately your submission targets the root challenge, user needs, and core objectives.
* **Code Quality:** Evaluates how clean, readable, and well-structured the submitted code is.

### Medium Impact
*These parameters check how well your solution works under the surface. Doing great here steadily elevates your score, while a miss moderately lowers your standing.*
* **Security:** Ensures the code follows safe practices and avoids common vulnerabilities.
* **Efficiency:** Evaluates how well the code utilizes resources like time and memory.

### Low Impact
*These criteria look at the final layers of polish. Excelling here gives a small boost to your score, but you need them to get a perfect score.*
* **Testing:** Measures how easily the code can be tested, validated, and maintained over time.
* **Accessibility:** Evaluates how usable the solution is for diverse users and environments.
